What does "Danae" Mean?

by Princess Danae D. Silawan

Hello, my name is "Princess Danae D. Silawan"; it's quite a mouthful, isn't it? Well, you can just call me "Danae".But this introduction got me thinking, "Why is my name that?", So I once asked the ones who gave it to me in the first place, my parents. They said I was named after the ship my father was boarding as a seaman while my mother was pregnant with me. Now, their response lead to even more questions like "Why'd they name their baby after a boat?" or "If I'm named after a boat, then who named it and why?". All this newfound curiosity made me want to do a bit of research. I pulled out the encyclopedia from our classroom's mini-library since I didn't have any data that day and I was at school. I opened up the book and wiped out the dust it collected over time just to see that I picked up Volume 1 of the encyclopedia series that only features terms that start with the letter A. After multiple trial-and-errors to get the book I'm looking for, I finally found it and it says:
DANAE, in classical mythology, was the mother of the hero Perseus. SEE PERSEUS
